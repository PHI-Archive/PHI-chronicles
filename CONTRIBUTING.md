# Contributor Field Protocol
### Threshold Initiative for Paranatural Affairs
### Document Type: Operational Directive
### Clearance: Public

---

## Who May Submit

The Threshold Initiative accepts documentation from any individual who has witnessed, investigated, or gathered reliable evidence of a paranatural interaction.

No prior affiliation with TIPA is required.

---

## What May Be Documented

TIPA accepts chronicles covering the following interaction categories:

- **Location Interaction**: A recurring paranatural interaction tied to a specific place.
- **Entity Interaction**: An interaction involving a sentient non-human presence. This includes but is not limited to Jinn, Yokai, Dokkaebi, and all regional equivalents. Any sentient presence capable of intentional good or harm qualifies for documentation regardless of its local designation.
- **Object Interaction**: An interaction associated with a physical object.
- **Event Interaction**: A recurring or spontaneous paranatural phenomenon.

Chronicles may document more than one category where evidence supports it.

---

## What May Not Be Documented

The following are outside the scope of TIPA's mandate and will not be accepted under any circumstances:

- Any interaction framed as divine, holy, or involving a supreme being or deity in any religious tradition.
- Any entity presented as an angel or equivalent divine messenger.
- Any chronicle whose primary subject is the nature of the devine.

TIPA does not investigate the divine. TIPA documents interactions.

Submissions of this nature will be closed without review.

---

## How To Submit

Prepare your chronicle according to the formatting standards outlined below and submit it as a **Pull Request** to the `main` branch.

Your submission will be reviewed by the Archive. If it meets TIPA's standards, it will be merged into the official record. If it does not, it will be closed.

No explanation will be provided for rejected submissions.

---

## Chronicle Formatting Standards

All chronicles must follow this structure:

```
# PHI-[NUMBER] — [TITLE]

**Category:** [Location / Entity / Object / Event] Interaction  
**Risk Level:** [Negligible / Low / Moderate / High / Critical]  
**Reliability Index:** [A / B / C / D / X]  
**Status:** [Active / Pending / Closed]  
**Location:** [Region, Country]  

---

## Summary

[A concise factual account of the interaction. No interpretation. No conclusions beyond what the evidence supports.]

---

## Records

[List attached records using the standard suffix format]
- pHI-[NUMBER]-INT01
- PHI-[NUMBER]-I01
- PHI-[NUMBER]-PR01
etc.

---

## Notes

[Optional. Additional observations from the investigating party.]
```

---

## Language Policy

Witness statements must be recorded in their original language whenever possible.

Translations are supplementary. They do not replace the original testimony.

If you are submitting a chronicle involving testimony in a language you do not speak, note this clearly. TIPA will seek independent translation before the record is finalized.

---

## Standards of Documentation

TIPA operates under a strict policy of neutrality.

- Do not determine what caused the interaction.
- Do not confirm or deny the nature of any entity.
- Do not insert personal belief, interpretation, or conclusion unless it is clearly labeled as such and supported by evidence.
- Record what happened. Preserve what remains.
