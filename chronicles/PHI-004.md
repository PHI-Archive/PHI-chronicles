# PHI-004: The Woman at the Window

### Category: Location Interaction
### Risk Level: Low
### Reliability Index: C
### Status: Closed
### Location: Rural village, Fès-Meknès, Morocco

---

## Summary

PHI-004 refers to recurring nighttime reports made by a minor residing in a rural household on the outskirts of a small village in the Fès-Meknès region.

The witness claimed that an elderly woman appeared outside the residence each night after sunset and remained near the window of the room in which the witness slept. According to the witness, the figure did not enter the home, did not speak loudly, and was visible only from the waist up. The witness stated that the figure returned repeatedly over a period of approximately two weeks.

The witness was an orphan and lived with her maternal uncle, who denied any paranatural activity and attributed the reports to "fatigue," "fear," and "brain issues." At the time of investigation, the witness displayed signs of severe emotional distress, erratic speech, and possible sleep deprivation. Investigators also noted physical marks on the witness consistent with prolonged neglect or abuse.

Several faded marks were observed on the exterior wall beneath the bedroom window. Their pattern was recorded and photographed, but no conclusive paranatural attribution could be established. The marks were not present in older municipal photographs of the property and were not reproduced during later inspections.

After review, TIPA personnel concluded that the available material did not meet the evidentiary threshold required to confirm a paranatural interaction. The case was closed as unsubstantiated.

The witness remained in the custody of her uncle following closure of the file.

## Notes

Field investigators reported that the witness became visibly calmer when removed from the residence during interviews, but later reverted to agitation after returning home.

No further monitoring was authorized after dismissal.

The final case note submitted by the lead investigator reads as follows:

«"The report may be false. The injuries are not."»
