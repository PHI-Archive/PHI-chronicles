# PHI-004-PR01

**Type:** Police Record  
**Source:** Local Gendarmerie Log  
**Date:** 17 March 2019  
**Location:** Rural village, Fès-Meknès, Morocco

---

## Summary

On the evening of 17 March 2019, local authorities were contacted after a minor female reported repeated nighttime visits by an elderly woman seen near her bedroom window.

Initial responding officers found no intruder, no tracks in the yard, and no signs of forced entry.

The household was occupied by the witness and her maternal uncle. The uncle denied all claims of paranormal activity and stated that the witness suffered from anxiety and "brain problems."

Officers recorded visible bruising on the witness’s forearm and upper shoulder area, along with signs of neglect inside the residence, including insufficient bedding and a lack of basic personal care items.

No formal abuse charge was filed at the time of the inspection. The matter was referred to TIPA after officers noted that the witness’s account remained consistent across two separate interviews.

---

## Officer Remarks

One officer wrote that the witness “appeared frightened, but not deliberately deceptive.”

Another officer noted that the uncle was “hostile, evasive, and unwilling to permit a longer inspection.”

No paranatural evidence was recovered during the initial response.

---

## Disposition

Case transferred to TIPA field personnel for review.
